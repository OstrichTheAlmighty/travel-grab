# Interview 1 — Idea Validation

Purpose: Validate the core TravelGrab hypothesis — that trip planning is painful, current tools fall short, and travelers would value AI-generated itinerary variations they can refine.

Target: A mix of people across age, demographic, travel frequency, and group/solo travel habits. Aim for breadth, not depth, in this round.

Created: 2026-06-03

---

## Interviewer Notes

- Ask about **past behavior**, not future intent. "What did you do last time" beats "would you ever".
- Do not pitch TravelGrab. Let frustrations and workarounds surface on their own.
- Follow up with "why?" and "tell me more about that" rather than moving to the next question.
- Capture verbatim quotes where possible — they're more useful than paraphrased summaries.

---

## Questions

1. Tell me about the last trip you planned. Walk me through how you went from idea to booking.

2. Which parts of planning that trip took the most time? Which parts did you actually enjoy?

3. What tools or sources did you use — websites, apps, friends, an agent? Why those specifically?

4. Have you ever started planning a trip and given up, or settled for something you weren't excited about? What happened?

5. When you travel with others, how do you coordinate preferences and make decisions together?

6. Think of the last time you used a search engine or OTA (Booking, Expedia, Skyscanner, Google Flights). What was frustrating about the results?

7. Have you ever used an AI tool (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) to help plan a trip? What did you ask, and how did it actually go?

8. If a tool handed you a few complete trip options tailored to your stated preferences, what would it need to show or prove for you to trust it enough to book one?

9. How much would saving, say, five hours of planning be worth to you for a typical trip? Have you ever paid someone — a travel agent, planner, concierge — to do this work?

10. After receiving an initial trip proposal, what kinds of changes, swaps, or additions would you most want to be able to make to refine it?
